GEMS-PI

Summary

Initially this project was to help the G.E.M.S. (Goats Education Medicine Sustainability) Development Foundation non-profit (http://goatsfortheoldgoat.com/) in administering and configuring 115 android tablets for disconnected classroom use in South Sudan. It then expanded to building infrastructure for the delivery of educational media to the tablets and other classroom resources. My main contribution for the project was in assembling and augmenting some raspberry pi's with an image provided by World Possible (http://pi.worldpossible.org/) and additional storage, remote administration, and UPS capabilities.

Raspberry PI

Initially lecture recordings and other educational resources were going to be provided by reach back to a medical university outside of the country over WiFi and WiMax. Technical and political constraints forced a different approach, mandating a disconnected configuration. The main parameters were low cost, media storage size, ease of administration, and power out survival.
